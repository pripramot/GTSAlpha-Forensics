Acquisition of iOS Frequent Locations

iOS Frequent Locations (Older versions of iOS) or Significant Locations (Newer versions of iOS) are places that the iOS device has recently been, as well as how often and when they were visited.

It is important to be aware that very recent visits may take several days to appear in this data. Some data may also disappear over time.

On older versions of iOS, Home and Work locations are not set by the user. They are determined by the device based on time and location behavior. On newer versions of iOS, Home, Work and School locations are based off addresses set in the 'My Card' contact.

On iOS15 and above, only a basic summary and some recent records are available. Unfortunately, it isn't currently possible to delve into these further as on older versions of iOS.

If Location Services or Frequent Locations are switched off, then it will not be possible to document frequent locations. 

Locating Frequent Locations on the device

Switch on the iOS device and open 'Settings'.

1. Scroll down and select 'Privacy': 2. Select 'Location Services':



3. Scroll down to the bottom of the page and select 'System Services': 4. Scroll down and select 'Frequent Locations' or 'Significant Locations':



Recording and Documenting the Frequent Locations

Below are the instructions for recording and documenting iOS Frequent Locations. Please familiarise yourself with the procedure before proceeding to the next stage to take photographs.

Frequent locations are made up of three screens: 

  1. The 'Frequent Locations History' screen displays a list of regions that the device has been to recently

  2. The 'Region' screen displays a list of locations within the region that the device has been to recently

  3. The 'Visits Location' screen displays the data and time that the device was recently at that location

  1. Take a photograph of all the regions listed under 'History' on the 'Frequent Locations History' screen.
    If there are a lot of regions listed, then it may be necessary to scroll down and take more than one photograph.

  2. Select the first region from the listed regions on the 'Frequent Locations History' screen.

  3. Take a photograph of all the locations listed for that region on the 'Region' screen.
    If there are a lot of locations listed, then it may be necessary to scroll down and take more than one photograph.

  4. Select the first location from the listed locations on the 'Region' screen.

  5. Take a photograph of all the visits listed for that location on the 'Visits Location' screen.
    If there are a lot of visits listed, then it may be necessary to scroll down and take more than one photograph.

    Important: For older iOS versions, make sure that the visits location is noted down in the notes section of the 'Preview/Edit Photo' page for all 'Visits Location' photographs. This is necessary due to the location not being displayed on the 'Visits Location' screen. On newer iOS versions this is not necessary as the location is displayed at the top of the 'Visits Location' screen
  6. Return to the 'Region' screen and select the next listed location.

  7. Repeat steps 5 and 6 until all locations for that region have been photographed.

  8. Return to the 'Frequent Locations History' screen and select the next listed region.

  9. Repeat steps 3 to 8 until all regions have been photographed.

  10. The documentation of iOS Frequent Locations is now complete and can be viewed in the 'Report Information' section of the Preview.
